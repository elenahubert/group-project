CAREERS & COST OF LIVING


Here, we have broken down the two most relevant topics for Northwestern upperclassmen looking at the future: their choice of career and the cost of living in major U.S. cities. This should serve as a guide to undecided students for selecting a career and a city that fits the financial outcome associated with that career.



Careers

This pie chart illustrates the distribution of industries in which students find employment after graduation, displayed as a time series spanning from 2013 to 2023. Users can explore how industry trends have evolved over the decade.

This Mekko chart showcases the average starting salaries for the 11 most popular careers for Northwesten graduates, with relevant cities highlighted for each career path. This can serve as a snapshot into popular careers and salaries.

Cost of Living

This scatter graph orders the least expensive cities in the US for graduates to start out in. This can be used to find accessible post-grad metropolitan destinations based on projected salaries from the prior interactives on careers.

Also a scatter graph, this interactive details the most expensive cities for post-graduates to live in. Those with big city aspirations can compare their projected salaries to these high cost of living cities for a reality check.
